# Code of Conduct

QuestKit adopts the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1][cc], which is the
de-facto standard code of conduct in the open-source community. The full
text — including pledge, standards, scope, enforcement guidelines, and
attribution — is available at the link below.

- **Full text**: <https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/>
- **Plain-text version**: <https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/code_of_conduct.txt>

## In short

We are committed to making participation in QuestKit a respectful experience
for everyone, regardless of background or identity. We welcome contributors
of all skill levels and ask that interactions be friendly, constructive, and
focused on the work.

Things we encourage:

- Empathetic, kind, and patient communication
- Constructive technical feedback offered and received gracefully
- Crediting others' work and acknowledging different viewpoints
- Prioritizing what is best for the project and its users

Things that will not be tolerated:

- Insulting, demeaning, or personally targeted remarks
- Sustained disruption of discussions or contributions
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a
  professional setting

For the authoritative and complete list, refer to the linked Contributor
Covenant 2.1 text.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it
privately to **conduct@questkit.dev** (placeholder — update before public
launch).

All reports will be reviewed promptly and fairly. The project maintainers
are obligated to respect the privacy and safety of anyone reporting an
incident.

## Enforcement

Maintainers follow the Contributor Covenant 2.1 Enforcement Guidelines
(Correction → Warning → Temporary Ban → Permanent Ban), applied
proportionally to the severity and pattern of the conduct.
